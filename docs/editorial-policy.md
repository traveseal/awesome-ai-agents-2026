# Editorial Policy

Awesome AI Agents 2026 is a curated list. The repository is open to contributions, but inclusion is not automatic.

## Mission

Help builders, researchers, operators, and founders quickly discover useful AI agent tools without having to filter through noise, spam, or marketing pages.

## What makes a strong entry

A strong entry is:

- Directly related to AI agents or agent workflows.
- Publicly available and usable.
- Actively maintained or clearly relevant despite being stable.
- Described in neutral, factual language.
- Linked to an official source.
- Placed in the right section.
- Useful to a builder who is comparing tools.

## What makes a weak entry

A weak entry is:

- Mostly a marketing landing page.
- A generic AI SaaS with no clear agent-specific angle.
- A consulting service or implementation offer with no product or reusable tool.
- A very small fork with no meaningful differentiation.
- A duplicate of an existing entry.
- A speculative project with no usable demo, docs, or code.
- Written with hype instead of facts.

## Neutral language examples

Use descriptions like:

- `Open-source MCP server for connecting agents to business data APIs.`
- `Browser automation agent for running natural-language QA tests.`
- `Agent observability platform with tracing, evals, and prompt management.`

Avoid descriptions like:

- `The ultimate revolutionary platform for the future of AI.`
- `The best and fastest agent product in the market.`
- `Game-changing AI tool for every business.`

## Link policy

Use canonical links whenever possible.

Preferred:

1. Official GitHub repository.
2. Official documentation.
3. Official product website.

Avoid:

- UTM parameters.
- Affiliate links.
- Referral links.
- Temporary campaign landing pages.
- Link shorteners.

## Commercial projects

Commercial tools may be included if they are clearly relevant and useful. They should not receive special treatment, and their descriptions should stay factual.

Services, agencies, and implementation offers should usually be excluded unless they provide a concrete reusable product, API, platform, framework, or agent tool.

## Duplicates

If the same project is submitted multiple times, consolidate discussion into the oldest or clearest issue/PR and close the rest as duplicates.

## Maintainer discretion

Maintainers can decline a submission even if it technically fits a category. The list should remain useful, readable, and trustworthy.
